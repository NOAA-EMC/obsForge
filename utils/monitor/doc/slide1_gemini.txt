Slide Title: Relational Metadata Framework for Observation Systems
Subtitle: A Prototype for Unified Monitoring and Data Orchestration

I. Functional Overview
The application integrates three disparate operational needs into a single ecosystem:

Inventory & Workflow: Tracking obsforge task status and physical file assets in a unified registry.

Integrity & Quality: Automated structural validation and physics-based "sanity checks" (gross-error detection).

Visualization: A static-generated web interface for low-latency graphical browsing and automated reporting.

II. Technical Architecture: The Memory-Resident Registry
The system replaces traditional O(n) file-system traversal with an optimized O(log n) relational engine.

Scale: Tens of thousands of files resolved in ~0.1 milliseconds.

Mechanism: A compact 100MB SQLite database that functions as a memory-cached index.

Efficiency: Transitions metadata discovery from high-latency disk I/O to near-instantaneous RAM access.

III. The Schema: A Centralized Source of Truth
The database codifies knowledge that was previously fragmented across the environment:

Structural Encoding: Replaces thousands of sidecar YAMLs, hard-coded directory logic, and naming conventions with a formal relational map.

Deep Introspection: Captures the internal NetCDF variable structure, not just the file-system envelope.

Embedded Analytics: Pre-computes the "Big 7" metrics (min, max, mean, etc.) and physical constraints (e.g., Earth-relative temperature ranges) during ingest.

Subdued Speaker Notes (The "Pitch"):
"The prototype you see at the provided URL represents a shift in how we manage observational datasets. Instead of relying on traditional scripts to scan directories or parse thousands of YAML files—which can take minutes or even hours—we’ve consolidated the entire environment's 'logic' into a 100MB relational registry.

Because the database is small enough to reside in the system's RAM cache, we achieve 0.1-millisecond lookups for assets. This isn't just an inventory; it’s an active framework. By pre-computing variable statistics and encoding physical bounds directly into the schema, we can automate structural integrity checks and provide a high-performance foundation for the more complex Lagrangian computations that follow."
